The Rubric

How fidelity is scored.

One published, weighted 100-point rubric, applied identically to every watch against its specific original. It measures design closeness, not build quality — and it is never a claim that anything is a copy. The full rubric →

30/100
Dial & handset

Marker shape and layout, text placement, handset style, colour and lume — the things your eye reads first.

25/100
Case & proportions

Case shape, lug design, crown guards — and, heavily, whether the diameter and thickness track the original. Wearing 6mm too large costs points.

20/100
Bezel

Bezel type and insert — dive, GMT 24-hour, tachymeter, fluted — plus material and font.

15/100
Movement class

Whether the movement matches the original’s character: automatic vs a meca-quartz standing in for a mechanical chronograph, a GMT complication present or faked, hand-wind where it matters.

10/100
Spec parity

Water resistance, crystal and bracelet/clasp quality relative to what the original is known for.
